EECS 498 - Conversational AI Project: Melody

Conversational AI is a major design experience class in which students build a conversational AI assistant using Clinc's platform. My team decided to create a conversational assistant for Spotify called Melody. Melody can do basic music controls such as play, pause, skip, and next/previous. Clinc's platform is built modeling a state machine, giving them the distinction from other platforms such as Alexa in that the interaction with the agent is more conversational. In order to really take advantage of this feature, we decided to make Melody also recommend songs based on criteria given by the user, imitating the experience of interacting with a record store owner.

Built using: Clinc, Spotify API, Python

Learning A-Z - QR Student Login & Isolated Feature

During my internship at Learning A-Z, I was given the task of creating a new method of logging in for students using Raz-Kids. Previously, they only had the option of entering a text password or clicking on an icon as a password, but with this feature they can also scan QR codes. QR codes can make logging in easier for kids who don't know how to type, and also make logging in faster. All they need to do is hold the QR code up to the camera. The project here is an isolation of my feature from the LAZ codebase, and only encompasses the main idea of the feature: logging in. Much of this code is unused in the demo site, but is used in the actual feature.

Built using: Digital Ocean, LEMP Stack (Linux, Nginx, MySQL, PHP), AngularJS

Girls Encoded - Twitter Emergency Text Classifier

In this Girls Encoded program led by Rada Mihalcea, participants were given the opportunity to be paired with a research mentor at the University of Michigan. I was paired with Dr. Kummerfeld, a NLP postdoc, and was given a research project to build a text classifier that would help emergency responders filter tweets during disasters such as hurricanes, earthquakes, floods, etc. Tweets were labeled with categories such as priority level (i.e. low, medium, high) and the associated event (i.e. fireColorado, westTexasExplosion). This text classifier is powered by the python spaCy library and data curated by Text Retrieval Conference (TREC) Incident Streams track. Attached below is the research poster I made for the programs research symposium, showing the results of this classifier.

Built using: Python, spaCy, Tweepy

Multidisciplinary Project - Automatic Speech-to-Text Assessment

In this project sponsored by Learning A-Z, I along with other CS and UX students are building a tool that will allow teachers to automatically assess student recordings. In Raz-Kids, there is a specific assignment called "high frequency words" in which students record themselves saying a list of words. Then, our feature runs the recording through a pipeline of speech processing, compares the results to the golden standard, and displays the results in an effective report. In collaboration with the UX students, we have focused a lot of attention on what teachers want out of this feature. In order to understand our users better, the UX students performed user interviews and we have taken this feedback into consideration in designing the assessment and reports. In addition to the technical aspects of the project, MDP focuses a lot on following a professional process to complete the project including setting milestones, creating Gantt charts, presenting progress, writing reports, etc. Therefore, through this program, I have been exposed to many important administrative tasks.

Built using: AngularJS, PHP, MySQL
